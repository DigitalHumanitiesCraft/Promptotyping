# Distillate: LLM Summer School for DH 2025 (course site)

The course site for the LLM for Digital Humanities Research summer school carries the dating evidence for the paper's footnote that PRISM featured in the school's advanced-prompting session, and its committed wording fixes the event frame and the session's stated content.

## Core statements

- The site dates the summer school on Large Language Models for Digital Humanities Research to September 8-11, 2025. [[00_representation/documents/llmdh-summer-school-2025#^dates]] ^s1
- The site frames the summer school as covering LLM fundamentals, prompt engineering, and digital editions. [[00_representation/documents/llmdh-summer-school-2025#^scope]] ^s2
- The site names the summer school's instructor as a lecturer with Digital Humanities Craft OG. [[00_representation/documents/llmdh-summer-school-2025#^instructor]] ^s3
- The site's Day 2 session "Advanced Prompting Techniques" (14:00-15:30) covers PRISM, Kaskade and other advanced prompting techniques. [[00_representation/documents/llmdh-summer-school-2025#^advanced]] ^s4
- The site links the summer school's course materials to the portal ml-school.uni-koeln.de. [[00_representation/documents/llmdh-summer-school-2025#^host]] ^s5

## Terms

- **Advanced Prompting Techniques**: the site's heading for the Day 2 session that lists PRISM among its topics; the section around it is titled "Prompt Engineering & AI Engineering". [[00_representation/documents/llmdh-summer-school-2025#^advanced]]
- **ml-school.uni-koeln.de**: the domain the site gives as the summer school's course-materials portal; the domain belongs to the University of Cologne. [[00_representation/documents/llmdh-summer-school-2025#^host]]

## Open questions

- The paper's footnote calls the session "Advanced Prompt Engineering", whereas the site heads it "Advanced Prompting Techniques" inside a section titled "Prompt Engineering & AI Engineering"; the wording diverges while the referent is the same session.
- The site carries no explicit "Cologne" or "Köln" string; the Cologne location rests on reading the course-materials host domain uni-koeln.de as Universität zu Köln.
- The site names the instructor as an individual with Digital Humanities Craft OG and states no University of Graz affiliation; identifying that instructor with the PRISM method's author is a cross-source step for the claim layer.
- The page lists PRISM only as a session topic with slide and manuscript links; it carries no PRISM definition or content text, which live in the linked Google materials and are not ingested here.
