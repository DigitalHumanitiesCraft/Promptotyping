# Distillate: Revision audit A2, corpus audit (2026-07-23)

The audit checked two hypotheses about the paper's evidence base against the documented project repositories, circularity of the document-quality observation and the surplus value of explicit semantics, and reports what the record does and does not isolate.

## Core statements

- Across the documented projects, document depth and verification effort co-vary, and the one-day builds carry deep document sets as well; the audit found no project pairing deep documents with demonstrably low verification, and none the other way round. [[00_representation/documents/revision-audit-a2-2026-07-23#^cov]] ^s1
- The paper's limitations passage names the confound between method effects and model capability but not the project-internal one, that document depth and verification effort are the same practitioner's investment and therefore move with the total attention a project received; the record confirms that these quantities are not separable in it. [[00_representation/documents/revision-audit-a2-2026-07-23#^conf]] ^s2
- No documented failure case supports the statement that failures cluster where documentation was vague, since the project inventory and the worked cases present successes only; the negative pole the record does carry is the artefact whose thin design layer leaves it bound to inherited convention. [[00_representation/documents/revision-audit-a2-2026-07-23#^neg]] ^s3

## Open questions

- Whether semantically explicit source data yields a better artefact than an equally careful description of flat data would, which the audit reports the record does not isolate.
