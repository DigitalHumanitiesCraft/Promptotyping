# Distillate: Revision audit A1, transfer claim (2026-07-23)

The audit checked the paper's claim that scholars without hybrid competence can translate research data into artefacts with the method, and states how far the evidence carries that claim.

## Core statements

- The record does not support the unassisted production of epistemically valuable research artefacts from a scholar's own research data by non-hybrid domain experts; what it supports is that the method is teachable and its document types producible by such experts in guided settings, with effectiveness in unassisted third hands left open. [[00_representation/documents/revision-audit-a1-2026-07-23#^ceil]] ^s1
- The teaching-case passage of the paper stays at communicability and producibility and asserts no third-hand effectiveness, since a prototype called real means existent and functional and a working prototype means runnable in a guided workshop setting. [[00_representation/documents/revision-audit-a1-2026-07-23#^teach]] ^s2
- The paper's hedge on transferability covers the field axis of schema-regular disciplines and leaves the hybrid to non-hybrid axis uncovered, which is where an explicit falsification offer belongs. [[00_representation/documents/revision-audit-a1-2026-07-23#^axis]] ^s3
