# Distillate: PRISM prompt (GM-DH)

The GM-DH PRISM prompt file states, in its own terms, what the PRISM technique is and how it runs, and its committed wording carries the content the Genealogy dating claim rests on.

## Core statements

- The source defines PRISM (Parameterised Recursive Insight Synthesis Matrix) as a prompting technique that implements Tree of Thought reasoning using a matrix format. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^desc]] ^s1
- The source has the model work each task through a fixed sequence of steps under the PRISM method. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^task]] ^s2
- The source parameterises the reasoning by setting Thinking Type, Focus Area, Depth, and Timeframe before the matrix is built. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^param]] ^s3
- The source rates each thought branch on a 1-5 scale based on relevance, feasibility, and potential impact. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^rate]] ^s4
- The source has PRISM recursively apply its full methodology to sub-problems as nested matrices, haltable by a "stop PRISM" command, before a synthesis phase integrates the highest-rated insights. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^desc]] ^s5
- The source's synthesis step integrates insights from all levels of analysis and emphasises the highest-rated thoughts and their interconnections. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^synth]] ^s6

## Terms

- **PRISM**: Parameterised Recursive Insight Synthesis Matrix, the name the source gives its matrix-format Tree of Thought technique. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^desc]]
- **Thought branch**: an alternative labelled T1.1, T1.2, and so on, tracked in the matrix and carrying a 1-5 rating. [[00_representation/documents/gm-dh-prism-prompt-2025-01#^desc]]
