# Distillate: LLM-Based Agents for Software Engineering — A Survey (Liu et al. 2024)

A systematic survey of LLM-based agents applied to software engineering; the paper cites it in Section 2.5 as the reference for the agents that Spec-Driven Development frameworks orchestrate. Quotations are taken from the arXiv abstract page.

## Core statements

- LLM-based agents extend standalone LLMs by giving them the ability to perceive and use external resources and tools. ^s1
  > "Compared to standalone LLMs, LLM-based agents substantially extend the versatility and expertise of LLMs by enhancing LLMs with the capabilities of perceiving and utilizing external resources and tools." (liu-2024, Abstract)
- Such agents have been applied to software engineering with demonstrated effectiveness. ^s2
  > "To date, LLM-based agents have been applied and shown remarkable effectiveness in Software Engineering (SE)." (liu-2024, Abstract)
- The survey collects 124 papers and organises them from both a software-engineering and an agent perspective. ^s3
  > "In this work, we present a comprehensive and systematic survey on LLM-based agents for SE. We collect 124 papers and categorize them from two perspectives, i.e., the SE and agent perspectives." (liu-2024, Abstract)
- It frames the combination of multiple agents with human interaction as the promising direction for complex real-world SE problems. ^s4
  > "The synergy between multiple agents and human interaction brings further promise in tackling complex real-world SE problems." (liu-2024, Abstract)

## Terms

- **LLM-based agent for software engineering**: an LLM augmented with tool use and environment perception, applied to software-engineering tasks. [[10_distillates/publications/liu-2024-llm-agents-se-survey#^s1]]

## Open questions

- The survey maps agents by task and component; the specification-as-source-of-truth framing that the paper attaches to this citation belongs to the Spec-Driven-Development discourse and to Macedo 2026, not to this survey.
