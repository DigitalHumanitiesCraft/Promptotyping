# Distillate: Packaging research artefacts with RO-Crate (Soiland-Reyes et al. 2022)

RO-Crate is a lightweight, community-driven way to package research artefacts with machine-readable metadata using Schema.org/JSON-LD; knowledge/paper.md 3.3 names it the nearest FAIR-infrastructure relative of the method's frontmatter self-description practice.

## Core statements

- RO-Crate is an open, community-driven, lightweight approach to packaging research artefacts with their metadata in machine-readable form. ^s1
  > "an open, community-driven, and lightweight approach to packaging research artefacts along with their metadata in a machine readable manner" (soiland-2022)
- It is based on Schema.org annotations in JSON-LD to formally describe metadata. ^s2
  > "RO-Crate is based on Schema.org annotations in JSON-LD, aiming to establish best practices to formally describe metadata" (soiland-2022)
- An RO-Crate is a structured archive of all items that contributed to a research outcome, with their identifiers, provenance, relations and annotations. ^s3
  > "An RO-Crate is a structured archive of all the items that contributed to a research outcome, including their identifiers, provenance, relations and annotations." (soiland-2022)
- By applying "just enough" Linked Data standards, RO-Crate simplifies making research outputs FAIR while enhancing reproducibility. ^s4
  > "By applying "just enough" Linked Data standards, RO-Crate simplifies the process of making research outputs FAIR while also enhancing research reproducibility." (soiland-2022)

## Terms

- **RO-Crate**: a lightweight Schema.org/JSON-LD packaging of research artefacts and their metadata for machines. [[10_distillates/publications/soiland-2022-ro-crate#^s1]]
