# Distillate: Tool Criticism: From Digital Methods to Digital Methodology (van Es, Wieringa and Schäfer 2018)

Van Es, Wieringa and Schäfer argue that a rigorous inquiry into the tools used for research must be an essential element of the research process, naming this inquiry tool criticism and casting it as the move from digital methods to digital methodology; knowledge/paper.md Section 2.2 cites this alongside Koolen et al. as the corrective demanding reflection on what a tool does to the material. Quotes are checked against the PDF hosted on co-author Mirko Tobias Schäfer's own website; the abstract sits on the first page (p. 24).

## Core statements

- The paper argues that a rigorous inquiry into research tools must be an essential element of the research process, and names this inquiry tool criticism. ^s1
  > "Consequently, this paper argues for the development of a rigorous inquiry into the tools used for research to be an essential element of the overall research process. We dub this enquiry 'tool criticism'." (van Es, Wieringa and Schäfer 2018, p. 24)
- Tool criticism opens the path from digital methods to a digital methodology. ^s2
  > "Tool criticism paves the way to move from digital methods [32, 33] to 'digital methodology'." (van Es, Wieringa and Schäfer 2018, p. 24)

## Terms

- **tool criticism**: a rigorous, reflective inquiry into how research tools shape knowledge production, treated as a necessary part of method rather than a neutral instrument choice. [[10_distillates/publications/vanes-2018-tool-criticism#^s1]]

## Open questions

- The source frames tool criticism as scrutiny of the tools one uses; the paper turns the same reflex on its own method, adding the rejection of tool positivism. That self-application is the paper's.
