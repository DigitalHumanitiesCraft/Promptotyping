# Distillate: A Primer for Evaluating LLMs in Social-Science Research (Abdurahman et al. 2025)

A methodological primer of best practices for reproducible LLM use in social science; the paper cites it in Section 6.3 as related recommendations for reproducibility, aligned with its own relocation of reproducibility onto documents and process. Quotations are checked against the accepted preprint (OSF, doi:10.31234/osf.io/ag7hy), marked in press at Advances in Methods and Practices in Psychological Science.

## Core statements

- The primer offers social scientists essential recommendations for replicable and robust results when using LLMs. ^s1
  > "We provide authors of social scientific research with essential recommendations to ensure replicable and robust results using LLMs." (abdurahman-2025, Abstract)
- It grounds these in the need for transparent methodological reporting against the non-deterministic and continuously evolving nature of the models. ^s2
  > "emphasizing the need for transparency in methodological reporting and the challenges posed by the non-deterministic and continuously evolving nature of these models." (abdurahman-2025, Abstract)
- Its core reproducibility requirement is full transparency of prompts, code, data, model versions and settings, alongside handling of output randomness and prompt sensitivity. ^s3
  > "ensuring full transparency by providing all prompts, code, data, model versions, and settings to enable replication, as well as addressing LLM-specific challenges such as output randomness, prompt sensitivity, and potential biases." (abdurahman-2025, §Conclusion)
- It asks researchers to justify the model choice, including static versus continuously updated systems, and to disclose how proprietary model changes might affect conclusions. ^s4
  > "Researchers should justify their choice of model (e.g., static vs. continuously updated systems) and disclose how related technical constraints, such as proprietary model changes, might affect conclusions." (abdurahman-2025, §Conclusion)
- It requires validating LLM outputs against ground-truth human data or established benchmarks. ^s5
  > "validating results against ground-truth human data or established benchmarks to confirm reliability." (abdurahman-2025, §Conclusion)

## Terms

- **Reproducibility transparency (for LLM studies)**: the disclosure of prompts, code, data, model versions and settings that the primer treats as the precondition for replicable LLM-based research. [[10_distillates/publications/abdurahman-2025-primer-llm-evaluation#^s3]]

## Open questions

- The primer targets social-science studies that automate coding or simulate participants; the paper transfers its transparency recommendations to research-artifact generation, a neighbouring but distinct use the source does not address.
