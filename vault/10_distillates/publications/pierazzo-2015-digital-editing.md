# Distillate: Digital Scholarly Editing (Pierazzo 2015)

Pierazzo argues that the interface of a digital edition is not a neutral display but carries editorial decisions and the editor's interpretation; knowledge/paper.md Section 5.3 cites this for its Edition Interfaces category. Quotes are checked against the author's open-access HAL preprint (hal-01182162, dated 2014) of the 2015 book; page numbers refer to the preprint's internal pagination.

## Core statements

- Interfaces build meaning and include editorial decisions and principles. ^s1
  > "Interfaces build meaning and include editorial decisions and principles." (Pierazzo 2015, preprint p. 179)
- Where several orderings of a text are each defensible, it is the interface that as a regulative principle provides the version the editor considers most correct. ^s2
  > "it is the interface that as a regulative principle provides the version considered more correct by the editor." (Pierazzo 2015, preprint p. 178)
- User interfaces play a central role in transmitting the editorial vision of the edited texts. ^s3
  > "User interfaces occupy an important role in the transmission of the editorial vision of the edited texts." (Pierazzo 2015, preprint p. 15)
- The interface is crucial as a way of embodying editorial statements, which argues for recognising interfaces as part of the scholarly output. ^s4
  > "how crucial the function of the interface is as a way of embodying editorial statements and also the need to advocate for the inclusion and even recognize interfaces as part of the scholarly output." (Pierazzo 2015, preprint p. 180)

## Terms

- **interface (of a digital edition)**: not a neutral display layer but a regulative principle that embodies editorial decisions and interpretation. [[10_distillates/publications/pierazzo-2015-digital-editing#^s1]]
