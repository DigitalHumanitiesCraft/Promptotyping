# Distillate: PROV-A, a web-based tool for provenance as Linked Open Data (Mariani 2025)

The paper presents a client-side web tool that structures and publishes provenance as Linked Open Data, running entirely in the browser without server-side architecture and keeping a human-in-the-loop over automated extraction; it grounds the paper's Section 4.1 footnote as a same-venue precedent for the server-free artefact type.

## Core statements

- The tool is web-based and streamlines the creation and publication of provenance information as Linked Open Data. ^s1
  > "a web-based tool designed to streamline the creation and publication of provenance information as LOD." (mariani-2025, abstract)
- As a client-side application it runs entirely in the user's web browser, avoiding the complexities of server-side architectures such as hosting costs, database management, and system maintenance, which benefits resource-limited contexts. ^s2
  > "runs entirely within the user’s web browser, avoiding the complexities of server-side architectures, such as hosting costs, database management, and system maintenance." (mariani-2025, § 16)
- The design keeps a human-in-the-loop, with experts actively validating, interpreting, and correcting the AI-driven outputs. ^s3
  > "This highlights the need for a human-in-the-loop approach, where experts actively validate, interpret, and correct AI-driven outputs." (mariani-2025, § 3)

## Terms

- **client-side application**: an application that runs entirely in the browser, without server-side hosting, database, or maintenance. [[10_distillates/publications/mariani-2025-prov-a#^s2]]

## Open questions

- The tool is scoped narrowly to cultural-heritage provenance to permit its lightweight, client-side implementation; the source does not claim the pattern generalises to arbitrary research data, so its use as a precedent is by analogy of form.
