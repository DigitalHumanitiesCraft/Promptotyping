# Distillate: Do one thing and do it well (Schonhardt 2026)

The article derives from the Unix philosophy a best practice for sustainable digital editing, replacing the monolithic platform with small specialised filters chained through text-level standard formats; it grounds the paper's Section 4.2 point that the epistemic function determines interface design and that doing one thing well underwrites a typology of small, specialised tools.

## Core statements

- The article takes the Unix philosophy, in McIlroy's formulation, as its model: write programs that do one thing and do it well, that work together, and that handle text streams as a universal interface. ^s1
  > "This is the Unix philosophy. Write programs that do one thing and do it well. Write programs to work together. Write programs that handle text streams because that is a universal interface." (schonhardt-2026, § 33)
- Transferred to editing, the principle conceives the editorial process as a chain of transparently linked, specialised filters whose input and output stay addressable and manipulable at the text level. ^s2
  > "digitale Editionsprozesse nicht als in sich abgeschlossene Vorgänge, sondern als Summe von in transparenter Weise verketteter spezialisierter Filter zu konzipieren, deren In- und Output stets auf einer Textebene adressier- und manipulierbar bleiben muss" (schonhardt-2026, § 33)
- At the centre of the pipe stands not a monolithic processing block but decentralised, exchangeable units. ^s3
  > "Im Zentrum der Pipe steht damit nicht ein monolithischer Verarbeitungsblock, sondern dezentrale Einheiten" (schonhardt-2026, § 32)
- The best practice consists of four principles derived from Unix philosophy: modularisation, interoperability, redundancy / explicitness, and linking potential. ^s4
  > "develops a best practice for sustainable edition projects derived from Unix philosophy, consisting of four principles: modularization, interoperability, redundancy / explicitness, and linking potential." (schonhardt-2026, abstract)

## Terms

- **Verkettungspotential (linking potential)**: the priority of solutions whose text-level output can be re-chained and reprocessed, kept under one's own control rather than in closed platforms. [[10_distillates/publications/schonhardt-2026-do-one-thing#^s4]]

## Open questions

- The article argues for the principle in sustainable long-term editing against externalised platforms; it does not treat generated, question-specific interfaces, so the paper's typology reads the same design philosophy onto a different artefact class.
