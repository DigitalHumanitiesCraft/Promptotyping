# Distillate: FAIR Principles for Research Software (Chue Hong et al. 2022)

The community standard that adapts the FAIR principles to research software; the paper uses it in Sections 4.1 and 6.3 as the field's yardstick for the promptotype-as-artifact, where reusability with detailed provenance (R1.2) is the strong point and a persistent identifier (F1) is the missing findability condition. Quotations are taken from the open-access Scientific Data article (Chue Hong et al. 2022, doi:10.1038/s41597-022-01710-x), the peer-reviewed presentation of the FAIR4RS Principles v1.0 recorded under the CSL id's RDA DOI.

## Core statements

- The FAIR4RS Working Group adapted the FAIR Guiding Principles to create dedicated FAIR Principles for Research Software, treating software as a FAIR-eligible digital object. ^s1
  > "The FAIR for Research Software (FAIR4RS) Working Group has adapted the FAIR Guiding Principles to create the FAIR Principles for Research Software (FAIR4RS Principles)." (chuehong-2022, Abstract)
- Under Findability, the first principle requires a globally unique and persistent identifier for the software. ^s2
  > "F1. Software is assigned a globally unique and persistent identifier." (chuehong-2022, Results)
- The Reusable principle bundles usability and reusability, requiring that software can be executed and also understood, modified, built upon or incorporated. ^s3
  > "R: Software is both usable (can be executed) and reusable (can be understood, modified, built upon, or incorporated into other software)." (chuehong-2022, Results)
- Under Reusable, a distinct guiding principle requires detailed provenance of the software. ^s4
  > "R1.2. Software is associated with detailed provenance." (chuehong-2022, Results)
- The Working Group defines research software as artefacts created during or for a research purpose, distinguishing it from general software used in research. ^s5
  > "source code files, algorithms, scripts, computational workflows and executables that were created during the research process or for a research purpose." (chuehong-2022, Results)

## Terms

- **R1.2 (detailed provenance)**: the FAIR4RS requirement that research software carry a record of why and how it came to be and who contributed, the principle the paper maps onto its journal-and-document by-product. [[10_distillates/publications/chuehong-2022-fair4rs#^s4]]
- **F1 (persistent identifier)**: the FAIR4RS findability requirement that the paper identifies as the condition unpublished promptotypes typically fail. [[10_distillates/publications/chuehong-2022-fair4rs#^s2]]

## Open questions

- The principles are declared aspirational goalposts; the source does not assess any concrete tool, so the paper's uneven FAIR4RS reading of a promptotype rests on the SZD-HTR audit, not on this source.
