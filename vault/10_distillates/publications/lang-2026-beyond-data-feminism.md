# Distillate: Beyond Data Feminism. Towards ethical data work (Lang / Suárez Cronauer 2026)

The working paper adapts Data Feminism into a digital source criticism for data work, insisting that a dataset's provenance and the interests it encodes be interrogated because data emerges from structures of power; it grounds the paper's Section 6.4 limit that well-modelled data eases generation without discharging critical examination of how the data came to be.

## Core statements

- The paper argues that the digital humanities need a digital source criticism that examines both the historical sources and the data-creation process, with Data Feminism offered as the toolkit. ^s1
  > "Digital Humanities require a digital source criticism that considers both the historical sources themselves and the data creation process." (lang-2026, abstract)
- Far from making traditional humanities skills obsolete, the AI revolution makes critical data work more crucial than ever for the digital humanities. ^s2
  > "the AI revolution makes it more crucial than ever for the Digital Humanities to engage in critical data work." (lang-2026, § 6)
- Examining a dataset's history includes asking who created the metadata, whose interests the project serves, and what their goals were. ^s3
  > "it is important to consider who created the metadata, whose interests the project serves, and what their goals were." (lang-2026, § 8)
- Data generally emerges from structures of power and reflects societal hierarchies that are worth making explicit. ^s4
  > "data generally emerges from structures of power and reflects societal hierarchies inherently worth making explicit." (lang-2026, § 9)

## Terms

- **digital source criticism (for data work)**: a two-fold critical examination of how the sources came into existence and how they became data, extended by Data Feminist attention to power. [[10_distillates/publications/lang-2026-beyond-data-feminism#^s1]]

## Open questions

- The paper argues at the level of ethics and epistemology; it does not address generative artefact production, so its use as a limit on the paper's method is the paper's extension of a general claim about data work.
