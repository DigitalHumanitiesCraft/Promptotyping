# Distillate: Finished, for now. Unfinishedness as epistemic value (König 2026)

The article argues that in the digital humanities the provisional is not a defect but a knowledge-fostering value, reading digital knowledge objects as process rather than finished product; it grounds the paper's Section 6.1 move that locates the promptotype's disposability within a recognised epistemic value of unfinishedness.

## Core statements

- The article's thesis is that science is always in the making and never final, and that unfinishedness should be understood not as a deficit but as a principle that fosters knowledge. ^s1
  > "Science is always in the making, never final." (koenig-2026, abstract)
- Unfinishedness names an epistemic stance that understands provisionality in digital practices as a knowledge-bringing resource. ^s2
  > "Er beschreibt eine epistemische Haltung, die Vorläufigkeit in digitalen Praktiken als erkenntnisbringende Ressource versteht." (koenig-2026, § 3)
- Digital representation makes visible that knowledge is a process and not a finished product. ^s3
  > "Wissen ist damit Prozess und kein fertiges Produkt." (koenig-2026, § 2)
- The value is deliberately cultivated practice, using provisionality, revision, and changeability productively to open new questions, dialogues, and perspectives, rather than leaving projects merely incomplete. ^s4
  > "verstehe ich Unfertigkeit als gezielt kultivierte Praxis, in der Vorläufigkeit, Revision und Veränderbarkeit produktiv genutzt werden, um neue Fragen, Dialoge und Perspektiven zu eröffnen." (koenig-2026, § 12)

## Terms

- **Unfertigkeit**: unfinishedness as a cultivated epistemic value, the deliberate keeping-open of knowledge processes as a resource. [[10_distillates/publications/koenig-2026-fertig-vorerst#^s2]]

## Open questions

- The article values unfinishedness within the research process and in continuously extended publications; it does not treat a disposable prototype whose value is what it teaches a persistent document set, which is the paper's specific transfer.
