# Distillate: DEPCHA and the Bookkeeping Ontology (Pollin 2019)

The paper documents the Bookkeeping Ontology as a conceptual model built with domain experts in an iterative "deep-dive" process, and records the Group class arising from an unspecified-collective entry; knowledge/paper.md Section 2.6 uses DEPCHA and the ontology in the method's genealogy. This 2019 conference paper independently dates the deep-dive process and the Group class ahead of the 2025 dissertation.

## Core statements

- The Bookkeeping Ontology is a conceptual data model based on the REA model and CIDOC CRM, developed in an ontology-engineering process involving historians, software developers, and digital humanists. ^s1
  > "a conceptual data model based on the REA [Mc82] model and CIDOC CRM8, is developed in an ontology engineering process, involving historians, software developers and digital humanists." (Pollin 2019, p. 9)
- In an iterative ontology-engineering process the project team discussed source phenomena in so-called deep-dives, and in this way the classes bk:Group and bk:Tax were added to the ontology. ^s2
  > "the project team discussed such phenomena during so-called ”deep-dives”, trying to understand the sources together to further improve our knowledge domain. In this particular case bk:Group and bk:Tax were added to the ontology." (Pollin 2019, p. 13)
- The concrete phenomenon prompting bk:Group was an unspecified collective of participants, such as four farmers and a baker who equally pay a certain tax. ^s3
  > "the annotation of unspecified groups of participants, such as four farmers and a baker, who equally pay a certain tax." (Pollin 2019, p. 13)
- A shared common vocabulary eases communication in the project team and can be reused by other projects, so the model is criticised and extended through reuse. ^s4
  > "a common vocabulary makes communication in the project team easier and it can be reused by other projects." (Pollin 2019, p. 13)

## Terms

- **deep-dive**: an iterative, collaborative session with domain experts in which source phenomena are discussed to extend the knowledge domain. [[10_distillates/publications/pollin-2019-depcha-bookkeeping#^s2]]
- **Bookkeeping Ontology**: a conceptual model of historical financial transactions, based on REA and CIDOC CRM, built through the DEPCHA deep-dive process. [[10_distillates/publications/pollin-2019-depcha-bookkeeping#^s1]]

## Open questions

- The 2019 paper attributes the Group class to a group that pays "a certain tax" (four farmers and a baker); the 2025 dissertation's account of the same class cites a tax-record entry "4 farmers pay a tax". The two renderings differ in wording; both trace the class to a collective-actor entry in tax records.
