# Distillate: Trading Zones of Digital History (Kemman 2021)

Kemman adapts Galison's trading-zone concept to digital-history collaborations between historians and computational experts, analysing them along engagement, power relations, and changing practices, and identifies brokers who translate between the communities; knowledge/paper.md draws on the trading-zone framing in Sections 1 and 2.4. Quotes are checked against the De Gruyter open-access edition (OAPEN); page numbers follow the book's pagination.

## Core statements

- Kemman develops a model that analyses digital-history collaborations as trading zones, a concept Galison introduced for how two communities with different practices coordinate a joint enterprise. ^s1
  > "This concept was developed by the historian of science Peter Galison to describe how two communities with vastly different practices and discourses can interact and negotiate a joint enterprise." (Kemman 2021, p. 4)
- Galison defined a trading zone as an arena in which radically different activities could be locally, but not globally, coordinated. ^s2
  > "He defined a trading zone as “an arena in which radically different activities could be locally, but not globally, coordinated.”" (Kemman 2021, p. 4)
- Kemman analyses trading zones along three dimensions: engagement, power relations, and changing practices. ^s3
  > "Through such coordination a trading zone emerges which I analyse according to three dimensions. First, engagement as the extent to which the two communities come together to meet and interact." (Kemman 2021, p. 4)
- With digital infrastructures the structure of databases affects what historians can do and which questions can be pursued, which is what necessitates cross-disciplinary collaboration. ^s4
  > "with digital infrastructures, the structure of databases affects what historians can do and what questions can be pursued." (Kemman 2021, p. 5)
- Digital-history brokers perform infrastructuring to resolve the tensions that arise when infrastructures are negotiated between historians and computational experts. ^s5
  > "I argue that digital history brokers are essentially performing infrastructuring to resolve the tensions that arise when digital infrastructures are developed and negotiated between historians and computational experts." (Kemman 2021, p. 7)

## Terms

- **trading zone**: an arena in which two communities with different practices locally coordinate a joint enterprise without merging globally. [[10_distillates/publications/kemman-2021-trading-zones#^s2]]
- **digital history broker**: an actor who connects and translates between the historical and computational communities of a digital-history collaboration. [[10_distillates/publications/kemman-2021-trading-zones#^s5]]

## Open questions

- The paper's Critical Expert unites domain and computational competence in one person, whereas Kemman's trading zone and broker describe relations between two distinct communities; the paper's collapse of the trading zone into a single role is its own move, not Kemman's.
