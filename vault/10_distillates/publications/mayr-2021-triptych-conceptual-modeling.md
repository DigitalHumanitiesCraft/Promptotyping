# Distillate: The triptych of conceptual modeling (Mayr & Thalheim 2021)

Mayr and Thalheim characterise the conceptual model as a link between a language dimension of terms and an encyclopedic dimension of domain notions; knowledge/paper.md 3.4 borrows this modelling vocabulary to argue that a Promptotyping Document is a conceptual model of the project.

## Core statements

- The paper contributes an "anatomy" of conceptual models, proposing a signature that clearly distinguishes them from other model types. ^s1
  > "We propose a signature of conceptual models for their characterization, which allows a clear distinction from other types of models." (mayr-2021)
- It classifies the conceptual model as a link between the dimension of linguistic terms and the encyclopedic dimension of notions. ^s2
  > "We combine our proposal of a more transparent explanation of the nature of conceptual models with an approach that classifies conceptual models as a link between the dimension of linguistic terms and the encyclopedic dimension of notions." (mayr-2021)
- Conceptual models are widely discussed, especially in computer science, yet their potential is far from exploited. ^s3
  > "conceptual models are widely discussed in science and practice, especially in computer science, but that their potential is far from being exploited." (mayr-2021)

## Terms

- **conceptual model**: in Mayr & Thalheim's framing, a model dimension linking linguistic terms to the encyclopedic notions of a domain. [[10_distillates/publications/mayr-2021-triptych-conceptual-modeling#^s2]]
