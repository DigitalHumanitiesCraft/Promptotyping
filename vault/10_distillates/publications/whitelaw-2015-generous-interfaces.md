# Distillate: Generous interfaces for digital cultural collections (Whitelaw 2015)

Whitelaw contrasts the ungenerous search box with generous interfaces that show the collection and invite browsing; the paper draws on this in Section 2.1 (exploration as research) and Section 4.2, where the Exploration Interface category operationalises generous interfaces.

## Core statements

- Search is the dominant interface to digitised cultural collections yet cannot represent their abundance. ^s1
  > "Decades of digitisation have made a wealth of digital cultural material available online. Yet search — the dominant interface to these collections — is incapable of representing this abundance." (whitelaw-2015)
- Search is ungenerous: it withholds information and demands a query. ^s2
  > "Search is ungenerous: it withholds information, and demands a query." (whitelaw-2015)
- Generous interfaces provide rich, navigable representations of large collections that invite exploration and support browsing. ^s3
  > "Generous interfaces provide rich, navigable representations of large digital collections; they invite exploration and support browsing." (whitelaw-2015)
- By revealing a collection's complexity, a generous interface enriches interpretation by exposing relationships and structures within the collection. ^s4
  > "In revealing the complexity of digital collections, a generous interface would also enrich interpretation by revealing relationships and structures within a collection." (whitelaw-2015)

## Terms

- **generous interface**: a rich, browsable representation of a collection that reveals its scale and complexity and invites exploration without a query. [[10_distillates/publications/whitelaw-2015-generous-interfaces#^s3]]

## Open questions

- Whitelaw addresses cultural-collection interfaces; the paper's transfer to LLM-generated Exploration Interfaces is its own move.
