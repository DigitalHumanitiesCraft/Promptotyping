# Distillate: Pollin dissertation (2025)

The author's doctoral dissertation formulates scholar-centred design, walks the chain from requirements captured with domain experts to built research artifacts, and grounds the Bookkeeping Ontology and its classes in that process, supplying the origin material the paper's Genealogy claims rest on.

## Core statements

- Pollin formulates scholar-centred design as a systematic, iterative methodology that folds the practices, needs, and expertise of academic scholars into the development of scholarly tools and frameworks through structured engagement, evaluation, and refinement. ^s1
  > "Scholar-centred design is a systematic, iterative methodology that integrates the practices, needs, and expertise of academic scholars into the development of scholarly tools and frameworks through structured processes of engagement, evaluation, and refinement." (Pollin 2025b, p. 89)
- Within scholar-centred design he gives user-centred design two complementary roles, deepening the domain understanding that advances the Bookkeeping Ontology and producing the practical interfaces and visualisations historians need for historical financial records. ^s2
  > "this chapter examines how user-centred design (UCD) principles can enhance this practice in two complementary ways: first, to better understand the domain of historical bookkeeping and economic transactions for further development of the Bookkeeping Ontology, and second, to develop practical interfaces and visualisations that enable historians to work effectively with historical financial records" (Pollin 2025b, p. 89)
- He treats personas, epics, and user stories as the structured requirements-engineering instruments of the approach, deriving the personas from in-depth "deep dive" interviews with historians together with analysis of the historical sources. ^s3
  > "Personas, epics and user stories serve as structured methodological approaches to requirements engineering for historical information systems. The personas were developed based on two primary sources: in-depth interviews with historians ("deep dives") and analysis of historical material sources, supported by insights from Digital Humanities literature." (Pollin 2025b, p. 93)
- Epics decompose into user stories written in a fixed role-goal-reason template, which fixes how functional requirements are captured from the domain experts. ^s4
  > "These epics are then broken down into specific user stories, which provide concise descriptions of functionality from the perspective of the end user, following the format: "As [type of user], I want [some goal] so that [some reason]"." (Pollin 2025b, p. 93)
- The captured requirements feed the built artifacts, informing interface design, TEI XML and ontology modelling, and the RDF data workflows. ^s5
  > "These methodological approaches informed the development of interface design, TEI XML and ontology modelling, and workflows for RDF data transformation and creation (see Chapter 5.3)." (Pollin 2025b, p. 93)
- The requirements were elicited between 2017 and 2020 in DEPCHA sessions with domain-expert historians working on concrete financial sources. ^s6
  > "The DEPCHA project implemented the UCD framework through a series of requirements elicitation sessions with domain experts (referred to as "deep dives"). Between 2017-2020, these sessions analysed and modelled historical financial records with project partners and researchers" (Pollin 2025b, p. 92)
- The collaborative sessions surfaced recurring representation needs that shaped the Bookkeeping Ontology. ^s7
  > "The collaborative process revealed recurring representation needs that shaped the ontology." (Pollin 2025b, p. 92)
- The class Group originated in tax-record sessions, where entries such as '4 farmers pay a tax' motivated a representation for collective economic actors without individual identification. ^s8
  > "in examining tax records, historians identified entries such as '4 farmers pay a tax', leading to the class Group for representing collective economic actors without individual identification." (Pollin 2025b, p. 92)
- The class Right originated in the Rentrechnung (1718) sessions, where entries regarding grazing rights established the basis for a representation of economic resources. ^s9
  > "analysis sessions for the Rentrechnung (1718) revealed entries regarding "grazing rights", establishing the basis for Right as a representation of economic resources." (Pollin 2025b, p. 92)
- The class AgentMention, together with the property by, originated in Wheaton Day Book sessions, where entries such as 'ax dlvd By Puffer' grounded a representation of agency in economic exchanges. ^s10
  > "sessions focusing on the Wheaton Day Book revealed entries such as 'ax dlvd By Puffer', providing the foundation for the property by and class AgentMention, representing agency in economic exchanges." (Pollin 2025b, p. 92)
- In his account of the DIKW hierarchy Pollin lists the involvement of a cognitive agent, equated with human agency, among the factors that distinguish versions of the model. ^s11
  > "Over time, different versions of the model have been developed, emphasising different priorities and aspects, including concepts such as signal, wisdom, meaning, context, involvement of a cognitive agent (human agency) and complexity." (Pollin 2025b, p. 26)
- Following Rowley, he ties the ascent from signals to structured knowledge to human agency, so that greater involvement of the cognitive agent yields the cognitive structures that give data context and significance. ^s12
  > "Rowley (2007, 175) explains the DIKW hierarchy by introducing two key variables: human agency and degree of structure. These dimensions illustrate the progression from signals to structured knowledge, with human agency describing the increasing involvement of cognitive agents in this process. As the level of human agency increases, so does the degree of structure, evolving from basic detection of physical entities to sophisticated cognitive structures that provide context and significance to data." (Pollin 2025b, p. 35)
- He characterises information as representing reality and reflecting the relationship between a system or cognitive agent and its external environment. ^s13
  > "Primarily, it represents reality and reflects the relationship between a system or cognitive agent and the external environment." (Pollin 2025b, p. 32)

## Terms

- **Scholar-centred design**: the systematic, iterative methodology that integrates scholars' practices, needs, and expertise into the development of scholarly tools and frameworks. (Pollin 2025b, p. 89)
- **Deep dive**: the term the dissertation uses for a requirements-elicitation session with a domain-expert historian. (Pollin 2025b, p. 92)
- **Bookkeeping Ontology**: the ontology for formalising economic transactions in historical sources, developed through the scholar-centred process. (Pollin 2025b, p. 92)

## Open questions

- The passages ground the artifact targets of interface design, TEI XML, ontology modelling, RDF workflows, and visualisations. The paper's phrase "annotation guidelines" as a named artifact does not appear in these chapters; the dissertation speaks of guided annotations and annotation exercises rather than a distinct guidelines artifact.
- The dissertation dates the requirements sessions to 2017-2020 and carries the 2025 publication year; it states no separate internal date for the first formulation of the ontology classes.
