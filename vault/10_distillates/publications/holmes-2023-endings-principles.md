# Distillate: The Endings Principles (Holmes & Takeda 2023)

Holmes and Takeda report the Endings Project's practice of rebuilding digital projects as static, dependency-free sites to survive software obsolescence; the paper cites it in Section 4.1 as the sustainability rationale for the self-contained static artefact.

## Core statements

- The Endings team rescued over a dozen projects from software obsolescence by reconstituting them as entirely static, standalone websites with minimal dependencies. ^s1
  > "we have rescued over a dozen projects from death by software obsolescence and reconstituted them as entirely static, standalone websites with minimal dependencies." (holmes-2023)
- The principles forbid dependence on server-side software, prescribing a static site with no databases, PHP, or Python. ^s2
  > "No dependence on server-side software: build a static website with no databases, no PHP, no Python." (holmes-2023)
- They forbid dependence on external libraries or services. ^s3
  > "No dependence on external libraries or services: no JQuery, no AngularJS, no Bootstrap, no Google Search." (holmes-2023)
- Every page should still function even without JavaScript or CSS. ^s4
  > "Every page should still function effectively even in the absence of JavaScript or CSS support." (holmes-2023)
- The programme's aim was to turn high-maintenance "Tamagotchi" projects into zero-maintenance "Pet Rocks". ^s5
  > "Our Endings mission was to devise methods for turning our Tamagotchis into Pet Rocks." (holmes-2023)

## Terms

- **Endings Principles**: guidelines for durable digital projects built as static, dependency-free sites. [[10_distillates/publications/holmes-2023-endings-principles#^s2]]

## Open questions

- The principles were formulated for digital scholarly editions; the paper applies them to document-driven generated artefacts, a target form they did not originally address.
