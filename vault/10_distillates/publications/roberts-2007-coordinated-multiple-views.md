# Distillate: Coordinated and Multiple Views (Roberts 2007)

Roberts states the premise and technique of Coordinated and Multiple Views for exploratory visualisation, that users understand data better when they view it through different, linked representations; knowledge/paper.md Section 5.3 grounds its Exploration Interfaces category on this concept.

## Core statements

- Multiple Coordinated Views is an exploratory visualisation technique whose premise is that users understand their data better when they interact with it through different representations. ^s1
  > "Multiple Coordinated Views is a specific exploratory visualization technique that enables users to explore their data. In fact, the overall premise for the technique is that users understand their data better if they interact with the presented information and view it through different representations." (Roberts 2007, p. 61)
- The technique lets users see the same data in various forms and coordinates the interaction between the different views. ^s2
  > "They all allow the user to see the data in various forms, to manipulate the visual presentation in different ways, and also interact and coordinate the interaction between the different views." (Roberts 2007, p. 61)
- The guiding concept is to let the user have a dialogue with the data, forming insight through interaction. ^s3
  > "Principally the concept is to allow the user to have a dialogue with the data." (Roberts 2007, p. 61)

## Terms

- **Coordinated and Multiple Views (CMV)**: an exploratory visualisation technique presenting data through several linked representations whose interactions are coordinated, so that new relationships become perceivable. [[10_distillates/publications/roberts-2007-coordinated-multiple-views#^s1]]

## Open questions

- Roberts describes the technique in general visualisation terms; the paper applies it to LLM-generated research interfaces (CorrespExplorer's twelve coordinated views), which is the paper's instantiation.
