# Distillate: The Four Pillars of Research Software Engineering (Cohen et al. 2021)

An account of the Research Software Engineering movement and its four supporting pillars; the paper cites it in Section 1 (with Baxter et al. 2012) as evidence that the field institutionalised the domain-technical intermediary as a recognised profession, the human answer to the translation problem that Promptotyping addresses differently. The IEEE Software version of record is paywalled; quotations are checked against the author preprint (arXiv:2002.01035), which the header marks as the author's version prior to publisher changes.

## Core statements

- The Research Software Engineering movement, originating in the UK, exists to recognise and support the individuals who build research software. ^s1
  > "The Research Software Engineering movement, which started in the UK and has been built up over recent years, aims to recognise and support these individuals." (cohen-2021, Abstract)
- The people who write and maintain research software are increasingly treated as critical members of research teams rather than mere code writers. ^s2
  > "the people who write, maintain and manage this research software are increasingly seen as critically important members of research teams, rather than just "the people who write code"." (cohen-2021, §Introduction)
- The article frames research software work as a realistic professional career, supported by emerging RSE-specific roles and job descriptions. ^s3
  > "recent progress means the area can now be considered as a more realistic option for a professional software development career." (cohen-2021, Abstract)
- Its structural contribution is four pillars, Software Development, Community, Training and Policy, presented as the elements of comprehensive support for quality research software. ^s4
  > "we introduce "4 pillars" of Research Software Engineering that we see as providing the necessary elements to offer comprehensive, ongoing support for the development of quality research software." (cohen-2021, §Introduction)

## Terms

- **Research Software Engineering (RSE)**: the movement and emerging profession, begun in the UK around 2013, that recognises and supports the people who build research software as a distinct role. [[10_distillates/publications/cohen-2021-four-pillars-rse#^s1]]

## Open questions

- The source documents institutional support for the RSE role but not its reach; the paper's inference that this professionalised answer does not reach the individual researcher or small project is its own, drawn across the Section 1 source cluster.
