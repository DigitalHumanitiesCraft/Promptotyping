# Distillate: A Brief Taxonomy of Prototypes for the Digital Humanities (Ruecker 2015)

Ruecker distinguishes three kinds of prototype by the project they support, experimental, production, and provocative, and holds that an experimental prototype yields generalised knowledge rather than a product; knowledge/paper.md Section 2.2 uses this to position the Promptotype as a knowledge-bearing by-product rather than the deliverable.

## Core statements

- Ruecker proposes three categories of prototype, intended for experiment, development, and provocation respectively. ^s1
  > "In general, there are three categories of prototype, which are intended for experiment, development, and provocation respectively." (Ruecker 2015, p. 1)
- In an experimental prototype the goal is not to make a product but to produce generalised knowledge about an idea the prototype embodies. ^s2
  > "Second are experimental prototypes, where the goal is not to create a product but instead to produce a kind of generalized knowledge about an idea that the prototype embodies." (Ruecker 2015, p. 2)
- A production-driven prototype, by contrast, is refined through ever-more-polished versions toward a robust product or system. ^s3
  > "First are production-driven prototypes, which are intended for refinement from a rough early version through ever-more-polished intermediate versions, until the final stage is ready to serve as the basis for a robust product or system." (Ruecker 2015, p. 2)
- A shared advantage of design and the digital humanities is the acceptance that an object of study can be created for the purpose of addressing a research question. ^s4
  > "One of the advantages shared by design and the digital humanities is that both fields are willing to accept the idea of an object of study being created for the purpose of addressing a research question." (Ruecker 2015, p. 7)

## Terms

- **experimental prototype**: a prototype built to produce generalised knowledge about an embodied idea rather than to become a product. [[10_distillates/publications/ruecker-2015-taxonomy-prototypes#^s2]]

## Open questions

- The paper treats the Promptotype as a disposable, regenerable by-product of the documents; Ruecker's experimental category supports the "not a product" half of that claim but says nothing about regeneration from a documentation layer, which is the paper's addition.
