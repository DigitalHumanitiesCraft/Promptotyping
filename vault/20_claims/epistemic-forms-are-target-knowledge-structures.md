# Epistemic forms are target knowledge structures that organise inquiry by the kind of knowledge produced.

## Statement

Collins and Ferguson define an epistemic form as a target structure that guides inquiry by organising knowledge and the relations among its concepts, paired with an epistemic game of rules and strategies for building that form. This grounds the paper's Section 5 use of epistemic forms as the principle for classifying research-data interfaces by the kind of knowledge they help construct, rather than by data format or technique.
