# The Bookkeeping Ontology took shape from representation needs surfaced in the sessions, with the classes Group, Right, and AgentMention traceable to named source sessions.

## Statement

The collaborative requirements sessions surfaced recurring representation needs that shaped the Bookkeeping Ontology. Three of its classes trace to named sources. The class Group arose in tax-record sessions, where entries such as '4 farmers pay a tax' motivated a representation for collective economic actors without individual identification. The class Right arose in the Rentrechnung (1718) sessions, where entries on grazing rights established the basis for a representation of economic resources. The class AgentMention, together with the property by, arose in Wheaton Day Book sessions, where entries such as 'ax dlvd By Puffer' grounded a representation of agency in economic exchanges.
