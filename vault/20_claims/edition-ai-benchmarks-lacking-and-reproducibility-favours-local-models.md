# Benchmarks for AI-generated edition tasks are lacking, and a reproducibility standard from within digital editing calls for free, locally runnable models.

## Statement

The field lacks benchmarks to rigorously evaluate the quality and accuracy of AI-generated results for its specific tasks, and it voices a reproducibility standard, citing Reiter's point that evaluating non-local, API-based models is product testing rather than model evaluation, that free and locally runnable models are a condition of comprehensible, reproducible, and open research. This grounds two of the paper's discussion moves: in Section 6.2 the method answers the missing-benchmark problem by assigning the check to the human and the deterministic layer, and in Section 6.3 it locates reproducibility in the documents and pipeline against the standard's presupposition of local models, a tension the paper marks as real.
