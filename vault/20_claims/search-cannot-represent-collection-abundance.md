# The search box, the default interface to digital collections, is ungenerous and cannot represent their abundance.

## Statement

Whitelaw argues that search, the dominant interface to digitised cultural collections, is incapable of representing their abundance; it is ungenerous, withholding information and demanding a query before it shows anything.

## Support

- [[10_distillates/publications/whitelaw-2015-generous-interfaces#^s1]] — search cannot represent the abundance of digitised collections.
- [[10_distillates/publications/whitelaw-2015-generous-interfaces#^s2]] — search withholds information and demands a query.
