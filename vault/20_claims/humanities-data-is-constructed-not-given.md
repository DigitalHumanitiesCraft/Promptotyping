# Humanities data is a selectively constructed abstraction, not a given.

## Statement

In the humanities, data is not a neutral given but a manufactured, selectively constructed abstraction that stands in for an object of inquiry. Its production requires choices about what to collect and how to encode it, and calling source material "data" imports assumptions of discreteness, tractability, and reproducibility that sit uneasily with humanistic ways of knowing. Even providing heritage collections as data is an intentional act rather than a passive exposure of what already exists.

## Support

- [[10_distillates/publications/owens-2011-defining-data#^s1]] — data is always manufactured and created; the notion of raw data misleads.
- [[10_distillates/publications/schoech-2013-big-smart-clean-messy#^s2]] — humanities data is a digital, selectively constructed, machine-actionable abstraction.
- [[10_distillates/publications/posner-2015-necessary-contradiction#^s1]] — naming something data imports assumptions that do not match how humanists work.
- [[10_distillates/publications/collectionsasdata-2019-santa-barbara#^s2]] — treating collections as data signals an intention to design for computation.
