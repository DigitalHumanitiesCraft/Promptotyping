# Collaboration between historians and computational experts is a trading zone.

## Statement

Kemman analyses digital-history collaborations as trading zones, adapting Galison's concept of an arena in which radically different activities are locally, but not globally, coordinated. Two communities with different practices coordinate a joint enterprise locally, which Kemman analyses along three dimensions: engagement, power relations, and changing practices.
