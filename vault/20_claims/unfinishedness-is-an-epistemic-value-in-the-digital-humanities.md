# In the field's German-language discourse, unfinishedness is an epistemic value: the provisional expresses the dynamics of scholarly knowledge.

## Statement

The digital humanities have an explicit discourse that reads the provisional not as a deficit but as a knowledge-fostering value, understanding digital knowledge objects as process rather than finished product and provisionality in digital practice as a resource for insight. This grounds the paper's Section 6.1 claim that the promptotype's disposability sits within a recognised epistemic value of unfinishedness, so that the tension between the disposable process and the durable published artefact dissolves once making and made are distinguished.
