# The dissertation places a cognitive agent whose knowledge structures give data context and significance at the centre of information production.

## Statement

In the dissertation's account of the DIKW hierarchy the involvement of a cognitive agent, equated with human agency, is among the factors that distinguish versions of the model. Following Rowley, the ascent from signals to structured knowledge is tied to human agency, so that greater involvement of the cognitive agent yields the cognitive structures that give data its context and significance. Information, on this account, represents reality and reflects the relationship between a system or cognitive agent and its external environment.
