# Schöch separates smart data from big data along structure, cleanliness, and volume.

## Statement

Schöch posits two core types of humanities data. Smart data is structured or semi-structured, explicit, enriched with markup and metadata, and clean, and is therefore small in volume because its creation depends on human agency. Big data, by contrast, is relatively unstructured, messy, and large. The distinction turns on how the data is created, modelled, and analysed, not on volume alone.
