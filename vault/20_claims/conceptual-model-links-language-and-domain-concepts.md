# A conceptual model links a language dimension of terms to an encyclopedic dimension of domain notions.

## Statement

Mayr and Thalheim characterise the conceptual model as a link between the dimension of linguistic terms and the encyclopedic dimension of notions. This is the modelling-theory characterisation the paper borrows to call a Promptotyping Document a conceptual model of the project. The stronger paper claim, that such a model is semi-formal and rests on a community of practice's shared vocabulary (pp. 57-59), is operator-verified at page level but not grounded here, since the source body text was inaccessible to intake.
