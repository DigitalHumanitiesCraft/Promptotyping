# Provenance and the interests a dataset encodes remain the scholar's responsibility; well-modelled data does not discharge critical data work.

## Statement

Because data emerges from structures of power and reflects societal hierarchies, the critical examination of how a dataset came to be, its provenance, who made its metadata, and whose interests it serves, remains the scholar's responsibility, and the AI turn makes this critical data work more rather than less crucial. This grounds the paper's Section 6.4 limit: FAIR-grade, well-modelled data eases generation but does not relieve the scholar of interrogating the data's origin and encoded interests.
