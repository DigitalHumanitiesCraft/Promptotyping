# Providing collections as data is an intentional, community-scoped act, not a default.

## Statement

Cultural-heritage institutions have rarely designed collections or access with computational use in mind; treating collections as data signals a deliberate intention to change that. Such provision is scoped to identified user communities rather than to everyone, so stewards must be intentional about who a collection is designed for.

## Support

- [[10_distillates/publications/collectionsasdata-2019-santa-barbara#^s2]] — treating collections as data signals an intention to support computational use.
- [[10_distillates/publications/collectionsasdata-2019-santa-barbara#^s3]] — collections as data designed for everyone serve no one.
