# An experimental prototype produces generalised knowledge rather than a product.

## Statement

Ruecker distinguishes three kinds of prototype by the project they support, intended for experiment, development, and provocation. In an experimental prototype the goal is not to create a product but to produce generalised knowledge about an idea the prototype embodies. This category grounds the positioning of a prototype as a knowledge-bearing object distinct from a production deliverable.
